    Most people know that Pippa and Kate Middleton's brother, James Middleton, has a love of dogs, which he regularly showcases on Instagram. But fans of the family might not realize that James is also a big fan of beekeeping.

    In a new article written for the Daily Mail, James revealed that his family encouraged his love of bees in the best way. Kate's brother wrote, "I’d always harbored a longing to keep bees, but it wasn’t until I turned 24 in 2011 that the wish became reality."

    In an adorable move, the Middleton clan stepped in and helped get James started as a beekeeper. He explained, "my family—mum, dad and my sisters Catherine and Pippa—clubbed together to buy what for me was the most fantastic birthday gift imaginable. A delivery van arrived with a large buzzing box with the cautionary label: 'Live Bees.' Inside was the nucleus—the start—of my colony: 1,000 Buckfast bees."

    Impressively, James revealed, "I now have almost half a million bees in eight hives in a meadow at our family home, Bucklebury Manor in Berkshire." Basically, it would seem that the gift he received from his family all those years ago has completely transformed his life.
