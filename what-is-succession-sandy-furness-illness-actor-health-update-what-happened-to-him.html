    Where Is Julian Assange Now In 2022?

    Julian Assange is accused of treason for divulging national secrets of the United States. Priti Patel made the choice to permit Julian Assange, a co-founder of WikiLeaks, to be extradited to the United States. WikiLeaks quickly said that it would file an appeal with the high court against Priti Patel’s judgment.

    The matter was sent to the British home secretary last month after the U.K. Supreme Court ruled that there were no legal challenges to the assurances provided by the U.S. authorities regarding Assange’s anticipated course of action.

    Patel has given his approval, but WikiLeaks has already published a statement saying that it will appeal the decision. The confrontation, according to it, would continue after today. It is only the beginning of fresh legal conflict. We’ll appeal through the judicial system.

    The extradition case has persisted since since Assange was detained in the UK in 2019 and is now being held there. A British magistrate gave Assange respite at the beginning of 2021 after concluding that transferring him to the American judicial system would place him at a high danger of committing suicide. The U.S., however, was able to successfully appeal that decision.

    Julian Assange’s Wife Warns That His Health Is Decorating

    In an interview with ABC Radio National on Monday, Stella Assange asserted that while Julian Assange is still a prisoner at London’s Belmarsh prison, his health is fast deteriorating.

    She mentioned that in October 2021, the founder of WikiLeaks suffered a minor stroke. He was in poor health when he joined the prison, she claimed, despite the fact that the conditions there are horrifying and would harm anyone’s health.

    In light of this, she continued, His condition was getting worse, and we’re worried that he might have a catastrophic medical incident within Belmarsh jail at any point without being able to obtain emergency care because, in effect, that’s how jails work.

    A request for Assange’s extradition to the United States, where he will face charges on 18 counts of espionage and computer intrusion, was made on June 17 by U.K. Home Secretary Priti Patel.
