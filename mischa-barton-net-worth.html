    Mischa Barton net worth-American actress, Mischa Anne Marsden Barton was born on January 24th, 1986 at Queen Charlotte’s and Chelsea Hospital in Hammersmith, London.

    Barton was born to an Irish mother, Nuala Quinn-Barton, a producer, and an English father, Paul Marsden Barton, a foreign exchange broker from Manchester.

    Irish language instruction was provided by her maternal grandpa at Queen’s University Belfast. She has two sisters: the younger Hania and the older Zoe, the latter of whom practices law in London.

    According to Barton, she briefly attended St. Paul’s Girls’ School in Hammersmith before her family was relocated to New York City when Barton was five years old due to her father’s job.

    She preserved her British citizenship after becoming a naturalized American citizen in 2006. Through her mother, she is also qualified for Irish citizenship.

    After Sir Richard Attenborough directed her in Closing the Ring, Barton attended a summer short course called Acting Shakespeare at The Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London in June and July 2006. She graduated from the Professional Children’s School in Manhattan in 2004.

    Mischa Barton career

    Barton is a British-American actress who works in cinema, television, and theater. At the Lincoln Center in New York City, she made her theatrical debut in Tony Kushner’s Slavs! and played the lead in James Lapine’s Twelve Dreams.

    She made her acting debut in 1996 with a cameo on the American serial opera All My Children, and she voiced a character in the Nickelodeon animated series KaBlam! from 1996 to 1997.

    She played the lead in the drama Lawn Dogs (1997) alongside Sam Rockwell, marking her first significant acting performance.

    She made notable film appearances in films including M. Night Shyamalan’s psychological thriller The Sixth Sense (1999) and the romantic comedy Notting Hill (1999). She also acted in the 1999 independent crime drama Pups.

    Later, Barton acted as Evan Rachel Wood’s girlfriend on ABC’s Once and Again (2001) and in the indie drama Lost and Delirious (2001).

    She portrayed Marissa Cooper in the 2003–2006 season of the Fox television series The O.C., for which she won two Teen Choice Awards.

    Barton became well-known because to the part, and Entertainment Weekly named her the year’s “It Girl” in 2003.

    Since then, Barton has acted in the drama Closing the Ring, directed by Richard Attenborough, as well as the comedies St Trinian’s (2007) and Assassination of a High School President (2008).

    She made a comeback to television in 2009, appearing in the CW series The Beautiful Life, which Ashton Kutcher produced.

    She made a stage comeback in 2012 by appearing in the Irish version of Steel Magnolias. She additionally starred with Martin Sheen in the 2014 film Bhopal: A Prayer for Rain.

    She has received favorable reviews for her performances in independent movies, with Starcrossed (2014) receiving praise from the Los Angeles Times for her “standout” work.

    Barton was cast in The Hills: New Beginnings (2019–2021), an MTV series that is a revival of The Hills.

    She was chosen for a lengthy guest appearance in 2023’s relaunch of the Australian serial opera Neighbours on Amazon Freeview.
