    Gerry White Obituary, Death Cause – On Thursday, November 9, 2023, the town of Mannington mourned the loss of a beloved community figure, Gerry Wayne White, who passed away at the age of 71. Born on June 27, 1952, in Parsons, Gerry was the son of the late L. O. and Mary Kepner White. His life, marked by a dedication to coaching, education, and community service, left an indelible impact on the lives of those he touched.

    Gerry’s journey as a coach spanned decades, leaving an enduring mark on the North Marion Football Team from 1991 to 2008. Under his guidance, the team achieved the pinnacle of success, becoming State Champions in 1997. Gerry’s coaching prowess and leadership skills were further recognized when he was named Coach of the Year in the 1995-1996 season. His influence extended beyond victories and accolades; Gerry was deeply invested in the development and success of his players. Many of his former players went on to achieve greatness in their lives, a source of pride for Gerry that transcended the football field.

    Before his tenure with the North Marion Football Team, Gerry contributed to the world of football as a graduate assistant football coach at Fairmont State College. His coaching journey also included a stint as an assistant coach and defensive coordinator at East Fairmont High School. Notably, Gerry served on the coaching staff for the North-South Football Game, further showcasing his commitment to the sport and the broader community.

    As an educator, Gerry shared his knowledge and passion for physical education and driver’s education with students at East Fairmont High School from 1973 to 2001 and at North Marion High School from 2001 to 2008. His impact in the classroom mirrored his coaching philosophy — fostering growth, discipline, and a sense of purpose in his students.
