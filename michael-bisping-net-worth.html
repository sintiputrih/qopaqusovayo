    He started his MMA career in UFC in 2004, won his first UFC championship in 2016, and became the first English MMA player to achieve the feat.

    Likewise, he previously held the record of registering 20 wins in the UFC. In addition, Bisping got inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame in 2019.

    Michael Bisping | Career

    Bisping started his early MMA career by defeating Steve Mathews via submission at Pride and Glory: Battle of the Ages in 2004.

    Meanwhile, in 2006, he featured in UFC’s Ultimate Fighter 3 television reality show and won the finals by beating Josh Haynes via TKO.

    In the same year, he was lined up for a UFC Ultimate Fighter 4 series finale and won the match against Eric Schafer via TKO in 4 minutes and 24 seconds.

    Likewise, he tasted his first defeat when his compatriot from the Ultimate Fighter Rashad Evans won via split decision.

    After his first defeat, Bisping moved down to the middleweight category from Light heavyweight and won his first match against Charles McCarthy via TKO.

    Likewise, from 2011 to 2014, he lost four fights in UFC and won around five contests.

    In addition, from 2015 onwards, he found his mojo back and started to dominate fights and finally grabbed his first UFC title in 2016, fighting against Luke Rockhold.

    Finally, Bisping took retirement in 2018 from his professional MMA career.
