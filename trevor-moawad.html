    Trevor Moawad- Biography

    Trevor Moawad was an American-born novelist best known for writing the Wall Street Journal best-selling “it takes what it takes” book. Andy Staples co-wrote the book, which was published by HarperCollins. Trevor was a well-known sports psychologist and mental conditioning expert who predominantly worked with elite sportsmen such as pitcher Marcus Storman and Seattle Seahawks quarterback Russell Wilson, who was also Trevor’s best friend. He was also the CEO and co-founder of limitless minds, a company founded in 2018 with the mission of advancing the way of life and streamlining execution. Unfortunately, the author died on September 15th, 2021, after a two-year battle with cancer.

    Trevor Moawad- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Parents

    Trevor Moawad was born on July 12, 1973, and died on September 15, 2021, at the age of 48. He was raised in Washington and was the son of the late Bob Moawad, a well-known performance educator around the world. Bob Moawad was the first contributor to Chicken Soup for the Soul and the former president of the National Association of Self-Esteem. He attended Occidental College in Los Angeles, California, United States of America, where Barack Obama and Jack Kemp both attended.

    Trevor was an all-conference and two-sport athlete at this college. He earned a bachelor’s degree in politics and a master’s degree in education from there. He gave out the Trevor Moawad Leadership Award at his college every year to outstanding girls and males who dedicated themselves to making their teammates better on and off the field.

    Trevor Moawad- Professional Career

    He started his career as a top strategic advisor and mental fitness coach for some of the world’s best athletes, teams, and businesses. Trevor Moawad was dubbed the “greatest brain trainer in sports” by Sports Illustrated in 2017. He was also the president of the Mouawad consultancy group, which was founded in 2014 in Scotland, Arizona. Trevor held many positions before founding his company, including director of mental conditioning and director of a multi-disciplined IMG performance institute (at IMG Academies in Bradenton, Florida). He was a well-liked mental conditioning coach for professional athletes and mentored college students, and in 2018, he founded Limitless Mind with Russel Wilson (an eight-time pro bowl quarterback whose mental coach was also Trevor).

    Trevor also co-wrote his first book, It Takes What It Takes, with Andy Staples of The Athletic. The book will be released in 2020. Trevor’s consulting firm was formed largely to provide forward-thinking solutions to the world’s most pressing problems for the most motivated leaders. Trevor continued to be a thought leader in a variety of performance venues, appearing on global media channels, national, local, and regional publications like USA Today, ESPN, Fox Sports, The Herald Tribune, SI, NPR, and Outside the Lines.
