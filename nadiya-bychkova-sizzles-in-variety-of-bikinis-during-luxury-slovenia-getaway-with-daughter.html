    Nadiya recently put a unique spin on the show’s notorious curse by insisting it helps put an end to rocky relationships.

    The long-running show has developed an undesirable reputation for unravelling previously stable relationships, with more than a dozen celeb contestants embarking on new romances with their respective dance partners during its 17 year history.

    But Nadiya insists it only speeds up the inevitable by delivering the final nail to romantic partnerships that are already faltering.

    She told The Mirror: ‘If the relationship is strong, it’s strong. If it’s not maybe it speeds up the result which is not a bad thing. It’s a TV show. It’s a big thing with the curses.

    ‘But those ­curses are everywhere, in ­offices and any kind of work but because it’s not on the TV, people don’t talk as much about it and it is what it is.’

    The Strictly curse gained traction during the show’s inaugural series in 2004, with newsreader Natasha Kaplinsky getting close to professional dancer Brendan Cole while both were in separate relationships.

    More recently comedian Seann Walsh found himself in hot water after he was pictured kissing then-married dance partner Katya Jones during a boozy night out.

    The incident prompted further outrage after it transpired that the kiss took place on his girlfriend Rebecca Humphries’ birthday. Humphries publicly ended her relationship with Walsh shortly afterwards.

    However Nadiya insists she is fortunate that her fiancé, Slovenian footballer Matija Škarabot, doesn’t get jealous of her celebrity dance partners – among them Blue lothario Lee Ryan and former England goalkeeper David James.

    She said: ‘I’m glad to have a partner that understands. I’m sure it’s not easy for any husband or wife or ­partners but it’s my work.’
