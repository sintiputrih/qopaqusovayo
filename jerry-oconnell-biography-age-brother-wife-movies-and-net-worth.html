    Jerry O’Connell Biography

    Jerry “Jeremiah” O’Connell born February 17, 1974, is an American actor, director, and talk show host. He is best known for his roles as Quinn Mallory in the television series Sliders and Vern Tessio in the film Stand by Me (1986). He also starred as Joe in Joe’s Apartment (1996), Derek in Scream 2 (1997), Andrew Clements in My Secret Identity, Frank Cushman in Jerry Maguire (1996), Charlie Carbone in Kangaroo Jack (2003), and Detective Woody Hoyt on the drama Crossing Jordan.

    Jerry O’Connell Acting Career

    O’Connell started his acting career at a young age doing commercial work for Duncan Hines cookies. At the age of 11, he landed his first feature film role playing the role of Vern Tessio in Rob Reiner’s Stand by Me. He then appeared in the 1987 commercial for Frosted Flakes. From 1988 to 1991, he starred in the Canadian science fiction sitcom My Secret Identity as the teen hero who develops superhuman traits.

    While pursuing his degree, Jerry starred in the feature film Calendar Girl alongside Jason Priestley and Gabriel Olds. He appeared alongside Jay Mohr and Hilary Swank in the short-lived ABC sitcom Camp Wilder in 1992. During his Junior year, O’Connell auditioned for the television pilot Sliders which was filmed in Canada. He got the role of Quinn Mallory in the series, which ran for three seasons on Fox and two seasons on the Syfy Channel. During his fourth season, he served as the producer and is credited with writing and directing several episodes.

    He got more acting roles such as Jerry Maguire, Body Shots, Mission to Mars, Tomcats, Scream 2, and Kangaroo Jack. Aside from acting, he also has credits in screenwriting. He sold his first screenplay, for First Daughter, to New Regency in 1999 which was released in 2004 by 20th Century Fox-based Davis Entertainment. He served as executive producer and received a “story by” credit on the film, which starred Katie Holmes and Michael Keaton.

    From 2001 to 2007, O’Connell starred as Detective Woody Hoyt on the NBC crime drama Crossing Jordan. He played Hoyt in several episodes of Las Vegas. In 2004, O’Connell wore a diaper on Last Call with Carson Daly, during a mock commercial skit for the GoodNites bedwetting product. In 2005, he guest-starred in one episode of the animated series Justice League Unlimited and played Captain Marvel in the episode, titled “Clash”. He again played the role of Captain Marvel in the DC Universe Animated Original Movies short film Superman/Shazam!: The Return of Black Adam with George Newbern, who reprised the role of Superman. From 2007 to 2008 O’Connell starred in ABC’s Carpoolers.

    He co-wrote the video parody called “Young Hillary Clinton”, which he also appeared in. The video criticized Hillary Clinton’s 2008 primary campaign. On February 2, 2008, he hosted VH1’s Pepsi Smash Super Bowl Bash, which aired the night before Super Bowl XLII. It featured musical guests Maroon 5 and Mary J. Blige. He guest-starred on ABC’s Samantha Who? on April 28, 2008. He also in Fox’s Do Not Disturb, in late 2008 co-starring Niecy Nash. Fox canceled the show after only three episodes.

    O’Connell appeared in the 2009 thriller film Obsessed playing the role of “Ben”, the best friend and co-worker of the film’s protagonist Derek Charles played by Idris Elba. He also appeared in Eastwick on ABC, on which his wife, Rebecca Romijn, also starred and portrayed Derrick Jones in Alexandre Aja’s Piranha 3D. O’Connell is also a stage actor, he appeared on Broadway at the Golden Theatre in Seminar.

    The play was written by Theresa Rebeck and directed by Sam Gold. On June 4, 2012, it was announced that O’Connell would play the role of Herman Munster in a reboot of The Munsters, titled Mockingbird Lane. O’Connell went ahead and play Stuart Strickland on We Are Men, alongside Tony Shalhoub. O’Connell made an appearance in January 2020, in the Roundabout’s American Airlines Theatre’s Broadway revival of A Soldier’s Play as Captain Taylor. The cast was headed by David Alan Grier and Blair Underwood.
