    Johanna Griggs – Biography

    Johanna Griggs was born in Sydney, Australia on October 17, 1972. The Australian television presenter is most well known for hosting Seven Network’s Better Homes and Gardens for more than a decade. She originally began her career as a medaled competitive swimmer.

    As the host of Better Homes and Gardens, she worked alongside chef and food host Karen Martini. She first rose to fame in 1990 after winning the bronze medal in the 100m backstrokes at the Auckland Commonwealth Games. Her swim career virtually ended at 17 when she began a battle with chronic fatigue syndrome.
