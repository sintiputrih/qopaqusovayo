    Who is Neal Bledsoe?

    Neal Bledsoe is an actor and writer.

    Neal is known for his performance on the NBC series Smash as a character, John Goodwin.

    He is currently doing Television Film Coming Home For Christmas.

    He has been involved with the 52nd Street Project, a New York City theatre company solely established for the creation of new plays for, and often by, kids ages between 9 to 18.

    Neal Bledsoe: Birth Facts, Family

    Neal Bledsoe was born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada on March 26, 1981, to Mr. Bledsoe and Mahalia McKellar. She was raised in Seattle, Washington.

    Drew Bledsoe, a former NFL quarterback is his relative. But he is not related to Tempestt Bledsoe.

    Neal Bledsoe is a big fan of the Seattle Mariners and Seahawks. He enjoys baseball as much as any other baseball player or fanboy.

    Neal Bledsoe: Education

    Neal went to a co-ed private boarding school, Shawnigan Lake School.

    Neal Bledsoe also attended Idyllwild Arts Academy. The Academy is famous for its training in music, theatre, dance, visual art, creative writing, film, and interdisciplinary arts.

    Later, he graduated from the University of North Carolina School of the Arts, Winston-Salem, NC.

    Neal Bledsoe: Professional Life

    Neal who was in love with acting didn’t miss a single movie or a series that he was offered.

    He was first seen on Television in 2005.

    Neal Bledsoe played in films, Junction(2012), Sex and the City 2(2010), Revolutionary Road(2008), and Police State(2017).

    He played the character Quinn in Guiding Light. He also appeared in other television series, Gossip Girl, Ugly Betty, and Smash.

    Neal filmed many pilots for The CW and Lifetime.

    On-stage, Bledsoe did a comedy play The Little Dog Laughed in Seattle and Impressionism on Broadway.

    Neal Bledsoe: Achievement, Awards

    In 2012, Neal won Technical Award and Jury Award for Best Actor in Junction.

    He also won the Festive Prize in 2013 and Best Actor in Narrative Feature Film for West End in 2014.

    He won a Grand Jury Award for Best Actor in The Morning the Sun Fell Down(2016).
