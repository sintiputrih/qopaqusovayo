    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Lil Reese was born and raised in Chicago, Illinois, a city known for its vibrant music scene. He began his music career at a young age, gaining recognition in the local music scene before breaking into the mainstream.

    Breakthrough with “I Don’t Like”

    Lil Reese’s breakthrough came in 2012 when he featured on Chief Keef’s hit single “I Don’t Like,” which peaked at number 73 on the Billboard Hot 100. The success of this song brought Lil Reese to the attention of major record labels.

    Signing with Def Jam Recordings

    In 2012, Lil Reese signed with Def Jam Recordings, a major milestone in his career. This deal significantly boosted his net worth and provided him with a platform to reach a larger audience.

    Releases under Def Jam

    Under Def Jam, Lil Reese released several mixtapes and singles, including “Us” and “Traffic.” These releases further increased his popularity and contributed to his growing net worth.
